Tools

In-the-moment tools.

Simple practices for overwhelm, shutdown, anxiety, grief, and the moments when you need something real to do right now.

60-second grounding

Press both feet into the floor. Name five things you see. Exhale longer than you inhale. Let your eyes slowly scan the room.

When you feel numb

Rub your hands together, touch your arms, use warm water, or stand and sway. The goal is contact, not instant motivation.

When you feel overwhelmed

Write every open loop down. Mark what is urgent, what feels urgent, and what can wait. Your mind needs a place to put the noise.

When grief feels heavy

Hold a pillow to your chest, rock gently, and let grief have a body before you force it into meaning.

When shame attacks

Try: “That voice is not neutral. That is attack.” Then name one fuller truth your body can actually believe.

When you feel alone

Put one hand on your chest and say, “I’m here.” It may sound small, but contact matters.

Tools are not a substitute for care.

These practices are here to help you interrupt the spiral, come back into the room, and create a little more choice. For crisis, danger, or medical emergencies, seek immediate local support.
