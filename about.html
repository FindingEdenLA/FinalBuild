About Finding Eden.

A community-built healing vision for Los Angeles — rooted in accessibility, creativity, nervous system safety, and honest human connection.

The heart of it

Finding Eden is not trying to look like every polished wellness brand.

It is being built for real people — people who are tired, grieving, healing, starting over, or quietly carrying things no one can see.

The vision is simple and ambitious: a future community healing arts center in Downtown Los Angeles where movement, art, music, journaling, storytelling, and peer connection are free or accessible — especially for people who cannot afford traditional support or do not connect with clinical spaces.

Finding Eden is not a replacement for therapy. It is the space around therapy. The place people go to practice regulation, be witnessed, move their bodies, tell the truth, make something, breathe again, and remember they are not alone.

Founder vision

A grassroots project built from lived experience, not distance.

Finding Eden comes from the belief that healing should feel human. Not cold. Not performative. Not locked behind high prices or perfect language.

The founder’s vision is to create a space that feels like the kind of place many people never had: calm enough to regulate, honest enough to be real, and community-centered enough to remind people that healing does not have to happen alone.

This is a long-term build. The first chapter is bi-monthly, free or donation-based pop-up gatherings in Downtown Los Angeles in 2026. The larger chapter is a permanent healing arts center rooted in access, creativity, and community trust.

The future space

What Finding Eden is becoming.

Movement rooms

Somatic stretching, gentle body-based grounding, breathwork, restorative movement, and nervous system reset practices.

Creative studios

Art therapy-inspired workshops, expressive writing, music, collage, and hands-on activities that help people process without needing perfect words.

Community circles

Peer-led, trauma-informed spaces for story, grief, boundaries, identity, and emotional truth without fixing or forced positivity.
